<p><p>The phoropter market encompasses two primary types: manual and automatic phoropters. Manual phoropters require optometrists to adjust lenses and settings manually for vision testing, offering precise customization and a traditional approach. Conversely, automatic phoropters streamline the process, utilizing electronic controls and software to quickly and accurately determine refractive errors. This automation enhances efficiency and patient experience, making automatic phoropters increasingly popular in modern optometric practices while still valuing the control offered by manual devices.</p></p>
